Essential Steps to Take During a Windstorm Disaster:

  1. Continue listening to the weather bulletin, the instructions issued by the competent authorities, and the warnings issued about the possible occurrence of the insects that accompany hurricanes. These insects are considered among the worst causes of death
  2. Prepare enough equipment before the storm arrives to avoid the lack of time that hinders survival from such an incident.
  3. Leave flat areas that are vulnerable to hurricane waves.
  4. Moor your boat well before storms or movement arrive in a specific, safe area and leave it moored until the storm subsides.
  5. Close the windows well or lock them with a special tape, because the dangers caused by small windows are always caused by waste that hits them, and large windows break due to the pressure of the storm on them.
  6. Keep things secured outside the home that may be blown by the wind, such as: utensils used to collect waste, garden equipment and tools, children’s toys, pets, and any other things. These things must be secured or stored before hurricanes approach
  7. Work to store drinking water in clean containers, and in cooking utensils, because public drinking water may spoil or the public water network may be damaged
  8. Always keep a radio and spare batteries for it, because the radio will be the only means of communication. Also, cooking and lighting facilities will be necessary in the event of a breakdown in public services
  9. Fill your vehicle with fuel, because service stations may be down for many days or due to a power outage.

Essential Steps to Take During a Flood Disaster:

  1. Evacuate Immediately if Advised Follow local authorities'evacuation orders promptly. Delaying evacuation can put your life and the lives of first responders at risk. Have a pre-determined evacuation plan and know the routes to take. Ensure you have a full tank of gas and all necessary supplies packed in your emergency kit.
  2. Move to Higher Ground If evacuation is not possible, move to the highest level of your home but avoid attics without windows or escape routes. Floodwaters can rise rapidly, and you need to have a way to signal for help or escape if necessary. Roofs can be a safer option if you are trapped, as they make you more visible to rescuers.
  3. Avoid Floodwaters Floodwaters can be extremely dangerous. They may contain sewage, chemicals, and debris, making them hazardous to health. Fast-moving water as shallow as six inches can knock an adult off their feet, and just one foot of water can sweep away a vehicle. Always use alternative routes and heed road closure signs.
  4. Stay Informed Stay tuned to emergency broadcasts, weather updates, and local alerts via a battery-powered or hand-crank radio, mobile phone, or other communication devices. Authorities will provide crucial information on the flood situation, including areas to avoid, shelter locations, and safety instructions.
  5. Prepare an Emergency Kit Your emergency kit should be easily accessible and contain:
    Water: One gallon per person per day for at least three days.
    Food: Non-perishable items to last at least three days.
    Medications: Prescription and over-the-counter medications.
    Important Documents: Identification, insurance papers, and bank records in a waterproof container.
    First Aid Supplies: Bandages, antiseptics, and other necessary medical items.
    Other Essentials: Flashlight, batteries, multi-tool, blankets, clothing, cash, and hygiene products.
  6. Turn Off Utilities Before floodwaters reach your home, turn off gas, electricity, and water to prevent fires, electrocution, and water contamination. Know how to turn off these utilities safely and ensure all household members are familiar with the process.
  7. Document Damage After the flood, take detailed photos and videos of all damage to your property for insurance claims and disaster assistance. Document everything before starting cleanup to provide clear evidence to insurance adjusters and aid organizations. Keep records of repair costs and receipts.
  8. Clean and Disinfect Floodwaters can bring in mud, bacteria, and harmful chemicals. Once it is safe to return, clean and disinfect all affected areas and items. Use protective gear like gloves and masks during cleanup. Remove wet materials like carpeting and furniture to prevent mold growth, and disinfect surfaces with appropriate cleaning solutions.
  9. Seek Assistance Contact your insurance company as soon as possible to report the damage and start the claims process. Additionally, apply for federal, state, or local disaster relief programs. Organizations like FEMA and the American Red Cross can provide financial assistance, temporary housing, and other essential services. Reach out to local community resources for further support.
